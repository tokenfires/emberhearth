- **Published:** January 29, 2026

## Why This Matters for EmberHearth

Important for understanding how context affects EmberHearth's long iMessage conversations. Shows that increasing context straightens neural trajectories in continual prediction but not in structured few-shot tasks — LLMs dynamically switch strategies based on task structure.

## Key Findings

- Increasing context increases straightness of neural sequence trajectories in continual prediction
- Representational straightening correlates with improved model prediction
- In few-shot settings, straightening only occurs during explicit structural phases
- LLMs function like a "Swiss Army knife" — dynamically selecting strategies based on task structure
- Measured in Gemma 2 models across diverse in-context tasks
