- **Authors:** Leheng Sheng, Wenchang Ma, Ruixin Hong, Xiang Wang, An Zhang, Tat-Seng Chua
- **Published:** February 11, 2026
- **Source:** arXiv

- **Referenced in:** [AI Self-Corrects Its Reasoning Complexity](../youtube/discoverai/2026-02-16-ai-self-corrects-its-reasoning-complexity.md)

## Why This Matters for EmberHearth

Relevant to EmberHearth's Ralph Loop quality assessment. RLCER uses self-generated rubrics to evaluate chain-of-thought quality without human labeling — a potential mechanism for the assistant to self-assess reasoning quality.

## Key Findings

- Proposes RLCER: Reinforcement Learning with CoT Supervision via Self-Evolving Rubrics
- Self-proposed rubrics provide reliable CoT supervision without outcome rewards
- Outperforms outcome-centric RLVR methods
- Rubrics serve as useful in-prompt hints during inference, further improving performance
- Addresses reward hacking and evolving CoT distribution challenges
