## Why This Matters for EmberHearth

Relevant to EmberHearth's multi-agent architecture (task agents + cognitive agents). Shows that sequence models trained against diverse co-players naturally develop cooperative strategies via in-context best-response — a scalable path to agent coordination.

## Key Findings

- Training sequence model agents against diverse co-players naturally induces in-context best-response strategies
- No hardcoded assumptions or explicit timescale separation needed for cooperation
- Vulnerability to exploitation drives mutual shaping of learning dynamics toward cooperation
- Standard decentralized RL on sequence models with co-player diversity enables cooperative behaviors
- Demonstrates emergent cooperation in prisoner's dilemma and other game-theoretic settings
