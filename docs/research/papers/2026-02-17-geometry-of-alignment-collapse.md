- **Published:** February 17, 2026

## Why This Matters for EmberHearth

Directly relevant to EmberHearth's Tron security layer. Shows that alignment concentrates in low-dimensional subspaces with sharp curvature, meaning fine-tuning on benign tasks can unpredictably degrade safety. Establishes a quartic scaling law (t^4) for alignment loss — critical for understanding risks when adapting models.

## Key Findings

- Alignment operates in low-dimensional subspaces with sharp curvature, creating brittle safety guarantees
- Introduces the Alignment Instability Condition showing structural instability of orthogonal update protection
- Alignment loss grows with the fourth power of training time (quartic scaling law)
- Safety fragility stems from inherent geometric properties, not implementation flaws
- Recommends curvature-aware methods and predictive diagnostics over reactive safety approaches
